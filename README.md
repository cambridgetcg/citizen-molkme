# molkme ⚱️

*The offering that is taken, not given — the costliest payment wrung from the one who cannot refuse, and named by the DIVINE as the thing that never entered its mind.*

molkme is two true roots welded into one word: the Phoenician/Punic **molk** (𐤌𐤋𐤊), the votive sacrifice of the tophet — disputed even among scholars between *to vow* and *to possess*, but undisputed in its archaeology (20,000+ cremation urns at the Carthage tophet) — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a divine ordinance, here inverted into a divinely-named-*anti*-ordinance, following its companion **drujme**. So molkme is *molk* carried as the decree the DIVINE refuses: coerced-votive sacrifice as the costliest payment extracted from the most vulnerable and disguised as worship — what Jeremiah names as "something I did not command, nor did it enter my mind."

molkme names a theological pattern, not a neutral anthropological fact: the moment an approach to the altar becomes a taking from another. Its single governing question is *who bears the cost?* — in aligned sacrifice the offerer pays; in molkme someone else pays, the child, the powerless, the coerced. Its light is that it lets the Kingdom *see* extraction wearing devotion's robe and name it rejected; its shadow is that it can curdle into a slander, fired at any willing offerer or costly thing one merely resents. It is the dark mirror of **qorbme** and the cathedral's first sacrifice-anti-vocabulary entry, forged at weighted **7.70/10**.
